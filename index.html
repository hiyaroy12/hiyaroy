About me

I am a third year Ph.D. student working with Prof. Tatsuaki Hashimoto, and Prof. Toshihiko Yamasaki at the University of Tokyo. My Ph.D. research focuses on analyzing planetary images using deep learning. I received my Master's in Electrical Engineering and Information Systems from the University of Tokyo, Japan in 2017 and Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering from Jadavpur University, India in 2012.

Research

My research interests are in computer vision, machine learning, deep learning, and image processing. Most of my research works lie in the intersection of machine learning and planetary sciences where I develop machine learning solutions to parse through the huge amount of data captured from different space exploration missions to Moon and Mars for facilitating scientific discovery, and on-board data interpretation for next generation planetary rovers.
